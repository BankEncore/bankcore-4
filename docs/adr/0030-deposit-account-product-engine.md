# ADR-0030: Deposit account product engine

**Status:** Proposed  
**Date:** 2026-04-27  
**Decision Type:** Deposits / Products architecture  
**Aligns with:** [ADR-0002](0002-operational-event-model.md), [ADR-0003](0003-posting-journal-architecture.md), [ADR-0004](0004-account-balance-model.md), [ADR-0005](0005-product-configuration-framework.md), [ADR-0012](0012-posting-rule-registry-and-journal-subledger.md), [ADR-0013](0013-holds-available-and-servicing-events.md), [ADR-0017](0017-deposit-products-fk-narrow-scope.md), [ADR-0019](0019-event-catalog-and-fee-events.md), [ADR-0021](0021-interest-accrual-and-payout-slice.md), [ADR-0022](0022-monthly-maintenance-fee-engine.md), [ADR-0023](0023-overdraft-nsf-deny-and-fee.md), [ADR-0024](0024-customer-visible-history-and-statements.md)

## 1. Context

BankCORE must support a broad deposit-account catalog, including demand deposit accounts, savings accounts, money market accounts, time deposits, and specialized deposit products such as IRA, HSA, trust, and business accounts.

The current implementation has the correct foundation:

- `deposit_products` is owned by `Products`.
- `deposit_accounts.deposit_product_id` is required.
- `deposit_accounts.product_code` is copied from the product at account opening.
- selected behavior is already product-resolved through `Products::Services::DepositProductResolver`.
- financial activity flows through operational events, posting batches, journal entries, and journal lines.
- holds and available-balance calculation remain owned by `Accounts`.
- statements are generated by the `Deposits` domain from ledger-backed activity.

This is intentionally narrower than the full product-configuration framework in ADR-0005. Existing deposit behavior still contains MVP compromises, including hardcoded GL account references in posting rules, caller-supplied interest amounts, and product behavior that is only partially represented as data.

BankCORE now needs a deposit-specific product engine direction that expands ADR-0005 for deposit accounts while preserving the event, posting, ledger, holds, and statement boundaries already accepted in earlier ADRs.

Decision drivers:

- support new deposit products without branching command logic on product codes
- keep financial truth in operational events, posting batches, and journal lines
- make product behavior auditable and reproducible
- preserve the existing `Products`, `Accounts`, `Deposits`, `OperationalEvents`, `Posting`, and `Ledger` domain boundaries
- allow phased migration from the current narrow product model
- avoid introducing a second ledger or bypass path around posting rules
- keep Reg CC, sweep orchestration, and full time-deposit servicing explicit future extensions

## 2. Decision

BankCORE will evolve deposit accounts toward a configuration-driven deposit product engine.

This ADR is a deposit-specific expansion of ADR-0005. It does not replace ADR-0017's narrow product FK slice; it defines the target architecture and migration path beyond that slice.

Deposit account behavior will be derived from product configuration resolved through stable product services. Commands may enforce account state, idempotency, business-date, channel, authorization, and operational safeguards, but they must not infer product behavior directly from `product_code` or hardcoded deposit account type strings.

The product engine evaluates product-driven behavior before financial posting. It does not post journals directly.

The financial boundary remains:

```text
User or system action
  -> OperationalEvent
  -> product and policy evaluation
  -> PostEvent
  -> PostingBatch
  -> JournalEntry / JournalLine
  -> deposit subledger reads from journal lines
```

For flows that must decide whether an action is allowed before an operational event is recorded, commands may call the product engine first and then record the resulting event, denial, hold, approval requirement, fee, or warning. The exact call order may vary by workflow, but posted financial activity must still be represented by operational events and posted through `Core::Posting::Commands::PostEvent`.

## 3. Deposit Categories

Every deposit product will have one canonical category:

```text
dda
sda
mmda
time_deposit
```

The category defines baseline regulatory and behavioral semantics only. It is not a product-code switch and must not contain the full product behavior.

Examples:

| Category | Baseline meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `dda` | Demand deposit account semantics. |
| `sda` | Savings deposit account semantics. |
| `mmda` | Money market deposit account semantics. |
| `time_deposit` | Term, maturity, renewal, and early-withdrawal semantics. |

Specialized offerings such as IRA checking, HSA savings, trust checking, nonprofit checking, business money market, or promotional CDs should be modeled as products within these categories plus additional eligibility, tax, ownership, and servicing rules where needed.

---

## 4. Product Behavior Model

Deposit products will define or reference behavior for:

- transaction eligibility and limits
- funds availability and hold policy
- interest calculation and posting policy
- fee assessment and waiver rules
- overdraft or NSF handling
- statement behavior
- account lifecycle behavior
- GL mapping profiles
- time-deposit maturity, renewal, and penalty behavior

The existing `Products::Services::DepositProductResolver` remains the narrow baseline resolver. It should evolve into a stable aggregate contract for deposit behavior rather than being bypassed by commands.

Initial resolved behavior families:

```text
DepositProductBehavior
  deposit_product
  deposit_category
  fee_rules
  overdraft_policy
  statement_profile
  interest_profile
  funds_availability_policy
  limit_profile
  gl_mapping_profile
  time_deposit_profile
```

Not every product category requires every behavior family. For example, a noninterest-bearing DDA may have no interest profile, and a non-time-deposit product may have no maturity profile.

---

## 5. Account-Level Product Contract

At account opening, BankCORE must preserve enough product information to reproduce the account's governing behavior for audit and customer-dispute review.

The target model is an account-level immutable product contract captured from the resolved product configuration at opening.

Conceptually:

```text
deposit_accounts
  deposit_product_id
  product_code
  product_contract_version
  product_contract_snapshot
```

The snapshot is the resolved account-governing contract, not necessarily a raw copy of every product row. It should include the effective product identity, category, selected behavior profile references or values, and any origination-time values that must not drift, such as a fixed CD rate or original term.

Rules:

- snapshots are append-only from the account's perspective
- commands evaluate against the account's governing contract unless an explicit servicing event changes the account to a new product contract
- product configuration changes must not silently rewrite historical account behavior
- account-level snapshots must be queryable for audit and support
- product versioning may coexist with snapshots, but runtime behavior must have one clear source of truth per account and business date

This ADR chooses the snapshot direction for deposit accounts, while leaving the physical storage shape to implementation. A JSONB snapshot is acceptable for an initial slice if it is validated, documented, and versioned.

## 6. Rules Evaluation Layer

BankCORE will introduce a deposit rules evaluation layer, for example:

```ruby
Deposits::RulesEngine.evaluate(action:, account:, amount_minor_units:, currency:, business_date:, context:)
```

The rules engine answers product and deposit-account questions such as:

- is the action allowed
- is approval required
- should a hold be placed
- is a fee applicable
- is a warning or exception required
- which product-derived limits or policies apply
- which downstream events should be requested by the command

The rules engine returns a decision object. It must not mutate balances and must not create journal entries.

Example decision shape:

```text
DepositRuleDecision
  outcome: allowed | denied | approval_required
  fees
  holds
  warnings
  exceptions
  rule_trace
```

Audit expectations:

- material rule evaluations should be logged or linked to the resulting operational event, control event, hold, approval, or servicing record
- rule traces must identify the product contract version or snapshot used
- denied or approval-required outcomes should be reproducible from recorded inputs and product contract data

The rules engine complements existing command validation. It does not replace idempotency checks, business-date validation, posting invariants, staff authorization, ledger balancing, or database constraints.

## 7. Event and Posting Boundaries

All financial activity must continue to flow through operational events and posting.

Deposit event families include:

- `deposit.accepted`
- `withdrawal.posted`
- `transfer.completed`
- `interest.accrued`
- `interest.posted`
- `fee.assessed`
- `overdraft.nsf_denied`
- future `overdraft.covered`
- future `time_deposit.matured`
- future `time_deposit.renewed`
- future `time_deposit.early_withdrawal`

The product engine may calculate amounts, required holds, fees, or warnings. It may not become a second posting engine.

`Core::Posting::Commands::PostEvent` remains responsible for converting posted financial events into balanced journal entries and journal lines.

The deposit subledger remains ledger-derived. Deposit balances must not be mutated directly outside the posting engine.

---

## 8. GL Mapping

The target architecture uses product-level GL mapping profiles to resolve posting accounts for deposit activity.

Profiles should cover:

- deposit liability accounts
- interest expense accounts
- accrued interest payable accounts
- fee income accounts
- overdraft or NSF fee income accounts
- time-deposit liability accounts
- penalty income accounts

This is a future extension of the current posting-rule registry. Until GL mapping profiles are implemented, posting rules may continue using the existing seeded COA conventions documented in ADR-0010 and related posting ADRs.

Migration rule:

- product GL mapping profiles must be introduced through a dedicated posting/GL ADR or implementation slice
- mixed hardcoded and profile-driven posting must be covered by tests that prove identical output for migrated products
- unresolved GL mappings must fail closed before posting

---

## 9. Interest Processing

Interest remains a two-phase financial process:

```text
interest.accrued
  -> recognizes expense and payable

interest.posted
  -> pays customer-visible interest into the deposit account
```

ADR-0021 remains authoritative for the current first slice: amounts are supplied by the system caller and posted through existing events.

The product engine target adds interest calculation policy:

- rate type
- day-count convention
- balance basis
- compounding
- accrual frequency
- posting frequency
- rounding and residual handling
- fixed-rate origination terms where applicable

Interest engines must emit rounded minor-unit amounts at the posting boundary. Fractional cents must not enter `journal_lines`.

---

## 10. Funds Availability and Holds

Funds availability policy may become product-driven, but `Accounts` remains the owner of holds and available-balance mechanics.

The product engine may determine:

- whether a deposit requires a hold
- hold amount
- hold duration
- reason code
- release schedule
- warning or exception state

The actual hold records, active-hold filtering, expiry, release, and available-balance calculation remain aligned with ADR-0013.

Reg CC schedules, collected-funds accounting, partial release ladders, and exception-hold workflows require future ADRs or implementation slices. They must not be smuggled into product configuration without updating the hold semantics and audit model.

---

## 11. Overdraft and NSF Handling

Overdraft and NSF behavior is product-driven.

The current deny-NSF policy and NSF fee flow remain valid. Future policies may include:

- deny with no fee
- deny with fee
- courtesy pay
- linked-account transfer
- overdraft line of credit
- approval-required overdraft

Each strategy must still produce explicit operational events and postings for financial effects. Linked-account, sweep, or line-of-credit funding flows should be orchestration workflows that call into the same event and posting kernel rather than special balance mutations.

---

## 12. Time Deposits

Time deposits are modeled as an extension of the deposit product framework, not as a separate ledger or account system.

Time-deposit products require additional behavior:

- term
- maturity date calculation
- fixed rate at origination
- early withdrawal penalty rules
- renewal instructions
- grace period
- payout instructions

Account-level product contracts are especially important for time deposits because rate, term, penalty, and renewal terms may be fixed at origination.

Future time-deposit events may include:

- `time_deposit.matured`
- `time_deposit.renewed`
- `time_deposit.early_withdrawal`
- `time_deposit.penalty_assessed`

## 13. Domain Ownership

Ownership remains:

| Layer | Owner | Responsibility |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Product configuration | `Products` | Deposit products, behavior profiles, effective dating, validation. |
| Deposit account state | `Accounts` | Account row, parties, status, holds, available balance. |
| Deposit rules evaluation | `Deposits` with `Products` inputs | Product-driven decisions for deposit-account actions. |
| Operational events | `Core::OperationalEvents` | Durable intent, idempotency, event catalog, traceability. |
| Posting | `Core::Posting` | Event-to-journal conversion and posting batches. |
| Ledger | `Core::Ledger` | Journal entries, journal lines, GL accounts, immutability. |
| Statements | `Deposits` | Customer-visible history and statements from ledger-backed activity. |

No domain should own a parallel account balance for deposit accounts.

---

## 14. Phased Implementation

Recommended migration path:

1. Add `deposit_category` to `deposit_products` and seed existing products as `dda`.
2. Define a versioned `DepositProductBehavior` contract returned by `DepositProductResolver`.
3. Add a validated product contract snapshot to `deposit_accounts`.
4. Capture the snapshot during `Accounts::Commands::OpenAccount`.
5. Introduce `Deposits::RulesEngine` for one narrow flow, preferably debit authorization or monthly fee assessment.
6. Record rule-evaluation traces for that flow.
7. Expand resolver coverage to funds availability, limits, and interest policy.
8. Add GL mapping profiles in a dedicated posting slice.
9. Add time-deposit profiles and events when a CD product is selected for implementation.
10. Migrate existing products incrementally with regression tests proving unchanged posting output.

This avoids a big-bang product-engine rewrite.

---

## 15. Consequences

Positive:

- new deposit products can be configured with less command-level branching
- account behavior becomes more auditable and reproducible
- product-driven fees, holds, overdraft policy, interest, and statements share one resolution path
- current operational event and posting architecture remains intact
- time deposits can reuse the same financial kernel
- historical account behavior is protected from silent product-rule drift

Negative:

- product configuration and validation become more complex
- account opening must persist and validate a product contract snapshot
- rule evaluation needs audit logging and test coverage
- product migrations require careful versioning and backfill strategy
- JSONB snapshots can become opaque unless governed by schemas and tests
- GL mapping migration has accounting risk and must be phased carefully

Neutral:

- effective-dated product rows may continue to exist alongside account snapshots.
- current hardcoded posting rules can remain during migration if they are documented and covered by tests.
- full Reg CC, sweeps, and CDs remain future implementation slices.

## 16. Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
| --- | --- |
| Product snapshot drifts from resolver behavior | Version the snapshot schema and generate it only through the resolver. |
| Commands bypass product rules | Route new product-sensitive behavior through `Deposits::RulesEngine` and add tests for critical flows. |
| Rule engine becomes a second posting engine | Keep the rules engine side-effect-light and require financial effects to flow through operational events and `PostEvent`. |
| GL mapping changes alter accounting output | Migrate one event family at a time with before/after posting tests. |
| JSON configuration becomes invalid or unreviewable | Add schema validation, admin validation, and seeded fixtures for baseline products. |
| Product versioning and account snapshots duplicate truth | Define account runtime behavior to use the account contract, while product versions govern new originations and explicit product-change servicing events. |
| Time deposits introduce special-case systems | Model CD-specific terms as product/account extensions and keep posting in the existing ledger kernel. |

---

## 17. Open Questions

1. Should the product contract snapshot store full resolved values, profile references plus version identifiers, or a hybrid of both?
2. Should product versioning be explicit on `deposit_products`, or implemented through immutable rows and effective-dated child tables?
3. Which flow should be the first `Deposits::RulesEngine` migration target: debit authorization, fee assessment, or account opening?
4. How much rule trace data should be persisted directly versus linked to operational events, holds, approvals, or servicing records?
5. Should GL mapping profiles be resolved by `Products`, `Posting`, or a dedicated shared resolver owned by `Core::Posting`?
6. What product attributes are required before the first savings, money-market, or CD product is introduced?
7. Should sweep behavior live inside product configuration, or in a separate orchestration layer that is enabled by product configuration?

---

## 18. Decision Summary

BankCORE will evolve deposit accounts toward a configuration-driven deposit product engine.

The engine extends ADR-0005 for deposit accounts while preserving the current operational event, posting, ledger, holds, and statement architecture. Deposit products define behavior, deposit accounts retain an immutable account-level product contract, deposit rules evaluation produces auditable decisions, and all financial effects continue to flow through operational events and posting.

This ADR defines the target direction and phased migration path. It does not claim that the current narrow `deposit_products` implementation already satisfies the full product-engine model.
